Title: Mary Visits Elizabeth — My, Your, His, Her

GRAMMAR

In this lesson, we learn four small words that help us say who something belongs to: my, your, his, her.
Mary and Elizabeth help us understand these words using names.

1. My — talking about myself

#IMG:imagem1.png
Mary points to herself and says: “My name is Mary.”
We use my when something belongs to me.

TableStart
Word | Meaning | Example
my | belongs to me | My name is Mary.
my | something from me | My baby, my family.
TableEnd

2. Your — talking to another person

#IMG:imagem2.png
Mary points to Elizabeth and says: “Your name is Elizabeth.”
We use your when we talk to you.

TableStart
Word | Meaning | Example
your | belongs to you | Your name is Elizabeth.
your | something from you | Your house, your baby.
TableEnd

3. His — talking about a boy

#IMG:imagem3.png
Elizabeth points to Mary’s belly and says: “His name is Jesus.”
We use his when something belongs to a boy.

TableStart
Word | Meaning | Example
his | belongs to a boy | His name is Jesus.
his | something from a boy | His book, his family.
TableEnd

4. Her — talking about a girl

#IMG:imagem4.png
Mary and Elizabeth see another woman nearby. Mary says: “Her name is Anna.”
We use her when something belongs to a girl.

TableStart
Word | Meaning | Example
her | belongs to a girl | Her name is Anna.
her | something from a girl | Her dress, her friend.
TableEnd
