Title: Mary Visits Elizabeth — My, Your, His, Her

GRAMMAR

Mary and Elizabeth were happy to see each other.
In this story, we learn four small words that help us talk about names and people we love.
These words are simple and friendly: my, your, his, her.

1. My — talking about myself

#IMG:imagem1.png
Mary arrives and greets Elizabeth with joy.
When I talk about something that belongs to me, I use my.

TableStart
Word | Meaning | Example
my | belongs to me | My name is Mary.
my | something from me | My baby, my family.
TableEnd

2. Your — talking to another person

#IMG:imagem2.png
When I speak to someone, I use your.
Mary asks Elizabeth: “How are you?” She is talking to her.

TableStart
Word | Meaning | Example
your | belongs to you | Your name is Elizabeth.
your | something from you | Your house, your baby.
TableEnd

3. His — talking about a boy

#IMG:imagem3.png
Elizabeth asks: “What’s your baby’s name, Mary?”
Mary answers with love: “His name is Jesus.”
We use his to talk about something that belongs to a boy.

TableStart
Word | Meaning | Example
his | belongs to a boy | His name is Jesus.
his | something from a boy | His book, his family.
TableEnd

4. Her — talking about a girl

#IMG:imagem4.png
If we talk about a girl, we use her.
Mary and Elizabeth could also talk about girls in their families.

TableStart
Word | Meaning | Example
her | belongs to a girl | Her name is Anna.
her | something from a girl | Her bag, her story.
TableEnd

5. Blessings and Goodbye

#IMG:imagem5.png
Mary and Elizabeth end their visit with joy and blessing.
Let’s remember:

TableStart
Word | Meaning | Example
my | belongs to me | My name is Mary.
your | belongs to you | Your name is Elizabeth.
his | belongs to a boy | His name is Jesus.
her | belongs to a girl | Her name is Mary.
TableEnd
