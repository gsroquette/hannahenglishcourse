Title: Mary Visits Elizabeth — My, Your, His, Her

GRAMMAR

Welcome! Today we learn small but powerful words that help us talk about names and people we love — just like Mary and Elizabeth did!

1. My name is…

#IMG:imagem1.png
When Mary arrived, she greeted Elizabeth with joy.
We use my to talk about something that belongs to me.

TableStart
Word | Meaning
my | something that belongs to me
my name is Anna | I say my own name
TableEnd

2. Your name is…

#IMG:imagem2.png
When we ask another person, we use your.
Mary asked, “How are you?” — she was talking to Elizabeth.

TableStart
Word | Meaning
your | something that belongs to you
your name is Ben | I say another person’s name
TableEnd

3. His name is…

#IMG:imagem3.png
Elizabeth asked Mary about her baby. Mary said: “His name is Jesus.”
We use his for a boy.

TableStart
Word | Meaning
his | belongs to a boy
his name is Jesus | Jesus is a boy
TableEnd

4. Her name is…

#IMG:imagem4.png
We use her for a girl.
If Mary were talking about a girl, she would say: “Her name is …”

TableStart
Word | Meaning
her | belongs to a girl
her name is Sara | Sara is a girl
TableEnd

5. Goodbye and Blessings!

#IMG:imagem5.png
Mary and Elizabeth said goodbye with love and blessings.
You can practice too:

TableStart
Sentence | Meaning
My name is Ana. | I say my name.
Your name is Leo. | I say your name.
His name is John. | A boy’s name.
Her name is Mary. | A girl’s name.
TableEnd
