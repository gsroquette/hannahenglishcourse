Title: A / An — God Made Everything

# GRAMMAR
God is good. God is the Creator. He made the world and everything in it.  
Look around! We can name what God made using small words: **a** and **an**.

## 1. What are "a" and "an"?
#IMG:imagem1.png
We use **a** and **an** with **one thing**.

- **a** = one (before most words)  
- **an** = one (before vowel sounds: a, e, i, o, u)

This is the world. God made everything. God is good!

TableStart
Word | Example
a | a tree
a | a rabbit
an | an elephant
an | an apple
TableEnd

## 2. Use "a" with consonant sounds
#IMG:imagem2.png
We use **a** before words that start with consonant sounds.

Look! God made the land and the animals.

We say:
- a tree  
- a rabbit  
- a person  

TableStart
Word | Sentence
tree | This is a tree.
rabbit | This is a rabbit.
person | This is a person.
TableEnd

## 3. Use "an" with vowel sounds
#IMG:imagem3.png
We use **an** before vowel sounds (a, e, i, o, u).

God made big and small animals.

We say:
- an elephant  

TableStart
Word | Sentence
elephant | This is an elephant.
apple | This is an apple.
TableEnd

## 4. One or Many
#IMG:imagem4.png
We use **a** or **an** for **one** thing.  
We do not use **a/an** for **many** things.

Look at God’s creation!

- one tree → a tree  
- many trees → trees  

TableStart
One | Many
a tree | trees
a star | stars
a person | people
TableEnd

## 5. Let's talk about God's creation
#IMG:imagem5.png
We can talk about what God made.

God is the Creator. God is good. God loves you!
