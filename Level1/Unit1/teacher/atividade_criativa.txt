**Interactive Activity: "The Garden of Choices"**

**Objective:**
Students will practice **Present Simple (affirmative, negative, and interrogative)** in a contextualized way, reflecting on choices, obedience, and consequences.

---

### **Step 1: Preparing the Activity**

- **Materials:**
  - Two cardboard trees placed on the wall:
    - One for **"Good Fruits"** (good actions - fruits of the spirit: love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, faithfulness, gentleness, self-control - **Galatians 5:22-23**).
    - Another for **"Forbidden Fruits"** (negative actions - acts of the flesh: lying, disobedience, selfishness, envy, pride, disrespect, laziness, ingratitude - **Galatians 5:19-21**).
  - Cards with images and phrases from the inspirational text.
  - Paper fruits that students will create, containing sentences about the story or their daily routines.
  - A poster with the question: **"What choices do we make every day?"**

---

### **Step 2: How to Play**

   - The teacher briefly presents the story of Adam and Eve, showing images.
   - Asks students: *"What choices did Adam and Eve have?"*
   - Students discuss and mention examples of daily choices.

   - Each student receives colored paper to create fruits.
   - They must write correct sentences in **Present Simple** (interrogative and affirmative or negative response) about the story or their daily routines. Depending on the response, the fruit will be placed on the correct tree (**Good Fruits** or **Forbidden Fruits**).

- **Good Fruit:**
  - *Do you always tell the truth?* ‚Üí *Yes, I always tell the truth to my parents.*

- **Forbidden Fruit:**
  - *Does he listen to his parents?* ‚Üí *No, he does not listen to his parents.*
  - *Does Eve obey God's command?* ‚Üí *No, Eve disobeys God's command.*
  - *Do they share their toys?* ‚Üí *No, they never share their toys.*
  - *Does she steal money from her friend?* ‚Üí *Yes, she steals money sometimes.*

üìå **Complete List of Keywords for Sentence Formation:**
- **Subjects:** I, You, He, She, It, We, They, Adam, Eve, The serpent, God, My family.
- **Auxiliary Verbs:** do, does.
- **Main Verbs:** listen, obey, disobey, help, lie, steal, love, trust, respect, work, eat, give, share, pray, read, study, go, wake up, sleep, talk, follow, choose, forgive, serve.
- **Complements:** to my parents, to God, the truth, every day, at school, in the morning, at night, before breakfast, after lunch, in the Bible, in the church, with kindness, with love, with patience, with honesty.
- **Time Expressions:** always, sometimes, never, every day, in the morning, in the evening, at noon, on weekends.

3Ô∏è‚É£ **"The Garden of Choices" - Quick Debate**
   - The teacher presents situations for students to reflect on, such as:
     - *"You see a friend cheating on a test. What do you do?"*
     - *"Your parents ask you to help at home, but you want to play. What do you choose?"*
     - *"Someone is being bullied at school. Do you help or ignore?"*
     - *"You find money on the floor. What do you do?"*
     - *"Your friend lies to the teacher. Do you tell the truth?"*
     - *"A new student has no friends. Do you talk to them?"*
   - Students respond using **Present Simple** and justify their answers.

---

### **Step 3: Reflection Moment**
- The teacher asks questions such as:
  - *"Why is obedience important in our lives?"*
  - *"How does this story relate to our daily choices?"*
- Each student writes a sentence about a good choice they can make this week.

---

### **Extra Challenge:**
Students create short dialogues in **Present Simple** between two characters discussing a choice. Then, they act it out for the class.

---

**Conclusion:**
This activity connects grammar, reflection, and interactivity, making learning engaging and meaningful!
