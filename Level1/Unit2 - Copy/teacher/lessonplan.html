StoryCards: Learning English with Biblical Stories

StoryCards are designed to teach English in a meaningful way, using Biblical stories that connect grammar, vocabulary, and textual comprehension. Below are the key pedagogical resources and suggestions on how to apply them in the classroom:

Pedagogical Resources of StoryCards

Contextualized Biblical Stories

  • Each story is carefully crafted to teach the grammar and vocabulary content of the unit.
  • Students learn English while exploring Biblical narratives, making the learning process more engaging and meaningful.

Connection Between Text and Image

  • The cards display illustrative images and texts, allowing students to associate vocabulary with visual elements.
  • Switching between text and image reinforces comprehension.

Pronunciation and Listening Comprehension Training

  • An integrated feature allows students to listen to the text being read aloud in English.
  • This exercise helps students practice pronunciation and improve listening skills naturally.

Structured Progression

  • Students must view and listen to all cards to complete the phase, ensuring a thorough and organized exploration of the content.

How to Apply StoryCards in the Classroom

Introduction to the Story

  • Begin by showing the images on the cards without revealing the text.
  • Ask students what they see and what they think the story is about, stimulating imagination and activating prior knowledge.

Vocabulary Exploration

  • Ask students to identify elements in the images and attempt to name them.
  • After exploring the images, reveal the text and read it with the students, highlighting the new vocabulary.

Grammar Focus

  • Use sentences from the text to explore specific grammatical structures of the unit (such as "there is" and "there are").
  • Create additional examples based on the context of the story.

Listening and Pronunciation Training

  • Activate the read-aloud feature so students can listen to the story.
  • Encourage students to read aloud, mimicking the pronunciation.
  • Divide the class into pairs and have each student narrate parts of the story to their partner.

Benefits for Students

  • Skill Integration: Develops reading, writing, listening, and speaking simultaneously.
  • Contextualized Learning: Grammar and vocabulary are learned in practical and engaging contexts.
  • Engagement: Images and stories spark curiosity and promote active participation.

With StoryCards, your lessons become more dynamic, interactive, and meaningful, connecting English learning with Biblical values and teachings. Happy Teaching! ðŸ˜Š

Flashcards: Interactive Vocabulary Building

Flashcards are designed to reinforce vocabulary learning using images and words in a dynamic and visual manner. Below are the pedagogical resources and suggestions on how to use them in the classroom:

Pedagogical Resources of Flashcards

Image and Word Association

  • Each card displays an image on the front and the corresponding word on the back.
  • Facilitates vocabulary memorization through visual connections.

Interactivity

  • Students can flip the cards to reveal the words, promoting a hands-on and engaging experience.
  • Direct interaction makes learning more active.

Integrated Audio

  • Cards feature an icon to play the audio of the English word.
  • Ideal for practicing pronunciation and reinforcing the association between sound, image, and written word.

Progression and Completion

  • Students must interact with all cards and listen to the audio to complete the activity.
  • Progress is automatically saved, marking the phase as complete once all cards are explored.

How to Apply Flashcards in the Classroom

Introduction to Vocabulary

  • Present flashcards as a tool to explore new words in the unit.
  • Ask students to identify the images and guess the words before flipping the cards.

Pronunciation Practice

  • Activate the audio so students can hear the correct pronunciation of the words.
  • Encourage students to repeat aloud to practice pronunciation.

Pair Activity

  • Divide students into pairs, where one describes the image and the other tries to guess the corresponding word.
  • Develops speaking and listening skills.

Benefits for Students

  • Multisensory Learning: Combines sight, sound, and tactile interaction to aid vocabulary retention.
  • Pronunciation Confidence: Audio practice helps students become familiar with English sounds.
  • Active Engagement: Flashcards make learning fun and motivating.

With Flashcards, your students learn vocabulary in a dynamic, interactive, and practical way, connecting words, images, and sounds intuitively. Happy Teaching! ðŸ˜Š

Grammar Exercises: Practice Makes Perfect

Grammar exercises are developed to provide students with interactive, meaningful, and contextualized practice, directly connecting the learned concepts to practical application. Below, we explain how Exercises and Fill in the Blanks work, highlighting their relationship with the Biblical story contents from StoryCards.

1. Grammar Exercises

Objective: Reinforce the grammatical rules learned by connecting them to the vocabulary and context of the unit.

Pedagogical Resources:

  • Fill-in-the-Blanks: Students complete sentences using the vocabulary and grammar learned in the context of the story.
  • Immediate Feedback: After each attempt, students receive information on what they got right or wrong, allowing them to review and try again.
  • Guided Correction: After a few attempts, the system provides the correct answer to support learning.
  • Integration with Story Text: Sentences and activities are directly based on the Biblical text presented in StoryCards, ensuring a connection between reading and grammar.

Classroom Application Tips:

  • Text Exploration: Before starting the exercises, reread parts of the Biblical text with the class, highlighting grammatical structures to be worked on.
  • Guided Practice: Solve one or two sentences as examples with the class to ensure everyone understands the activity.
  • Discussion of Answers: At the end, discuss correct answers and common mistakes, reinforcing the grammatical concepts worked on.

2. Fill in the Blanks

Objective: Practice the grammar and vocabulary learned within the context of sentences from the Biblical story.

Pedagogical Resources:

  • Contextualized Questions: Each sentence in the exercise is taken or adapted from the Biblical stories in StoryCards, showing real use of grammar.
  • Interactive Hints: The "Tips" button provides suggestions or clues to help students complete the blanks, encouraging self-sufficiency.
  • Read Aloud: The "Read" feature narrates the complete text with the correct answers, aiding in listening and pronunciation practice.
  • Simple Navigation: Students can switch between questions to review or confirm their answers, promoting continuous practice.

Classroom Application Tips:

  • Connection with StoryCards: Explain how the sentences come directly from the previously worked-on story and remind them of the context to support their answers.
  • Pair Activity: Have students discuss possible answers with a partner before filling in the blanks.
  • Listening Practice: After completing the blanks, encourage students to listen to the complete text and repeat the sentences aloud to reinforce pronunciation.

The Teacher's Role

  • Guide in Applying Rules: Assist students in applying grammatical rules and reinforcing the connection with Biblical texts.
  • Clarify Doubts: Correct mistakes and ensure students understand the connections between grammar and context.

Why These Activities Are Important

  • Significant Contextualization: Integrate grammar into the Biblical text, showing practical application.
  • Interactive Reinforcement: Gamification and immediate feedback make learning dynamic and motivating.
  • Preparation for Future Activities: These activities prepare students to consolidate learning with games, quizzes, and other dynamic practices.

With Exercises and Fill in the Blanks, your students practice grammar in a rich and meaningful context, developing comprehension, writing, and listening skills. Happy Teaching! ðŸ˜Š

Speak: Pronunciation Practice with Voice Recognition

Speak is an interactive tool developed to help students practice the correct pronunciation of learned words and phrases using voice recognition technology. Combining technology and pedagogy, Speak is designed to train speaking skills dynamically and personally.

How It Works

Guided Reading

  • Students view a word accompanied by an illustrative image.
  • An audio icon allows students to hear the correct pronunciation of the word in American English (US accent), training their ears to the correct sound.

Voice Recognition

  • By clicking the microphone button, students pronounce the word.
  • The system uses voice recognition to compare the student's pronunciation with the expected word.

Immediate Feedback

  • Correct: Positive feedback with messages like "Correct!" and motivational animations, such as a congratulatory GIF.
  • Incorrect: Prompts the student to try again, providing visual hints and repeating the word to reinforce learning.

Controlled Progression

  • Each word allows up to three attempts before moving to the next.
  • The system tracks practiced words, ensuring students complete the entire set before finishing the activity.

Pedagogical Objectives

  • Pronunciation Training: Encourage correct speaking using voice recognition as a validation and motivation tool.
  • Listening Enhancement: The read-aloud feature reinforces auditory learning, helping students identify specific English sounds.
  • Confidence Building: Immediate feedback reduces anxiety and motivates continuous practice.

How to Apply Speak in the Classroom

Introduction to Vocabulary

  • Review the words to be practiced, discussing their meanings and contexts.

Individual Practice

  • Students use Speak at their own pace, listening and repeating each word until they achieve correct pronunciation.

Group Discussion

  • After individual practice, facilitate a collective review.
  • Ask students which words were easier or more challenging.

Progress Review

  • Use system feedback to identify words that need more practice and propose reinforcement activities.

Benefits of Speak

  • Personalized Practice: Students learn at their own pace, adjusting the learning speed to their needs.
  • Positive and Motivational Feedback: Animations and congratulatory messages enhance engagement.
  • Multisensory Development: Combines audio, text, and speech for a comprehensive learning experience.
  • Progress Control: Tracking ensures all students practice and master every word.

With Speak, students have the opportunity to practice their speaking skills in a practical, fun, and effective manner, consolidating their learning and gaining confidence to use English in real-life situations. Happy Teaching! ðŸ˜Š

Dialogue and Conversation: Fluency in English with Robot Samuel

Conversation practice is a cornerstone of our course, designed to build students' confidence and fluency in English. Each unit has a specific theme, contextualized to provide real learning and practical language application. This practice is structured in four complementary stages, culminating in interaction with Robot Samuel, an AI tool that simulates a real conversation with the student.

1. Dialogue and Conversation: Theoretical Lesson

Objective: Introduce the unit's theme and teach essential dialogue elements such as vocabulary, grammar, and useful phrases.

How It Works:

  • The teacher presents the unit's topic in detail, explaining how to start, develop, and conclude a conversation on the theme.
  • Simple examples tailored to the students' level are used, such as greetings and introductions.
  • Example Theme: "Talking about Hobbies"
    • Sample Phrases: "I like to play football." or "My favorite hobby is reading."
  • Application Tip: Use this lesson as a foundation for subsequent activities, ensuring students grasp the theoretical content before practicing.

2. Dialogue: Practical Exercises

Objective: Reinforce theoretical content through interactive exercises like quizzes and Q&A activities.

How It Works:

  • Students engage in exercises that simulate parts of a conversation.
  • Questions and answers are presented, and students choose or complete the correct options.
  • Sample Question:
    • Q: How do you introduce yourself?
    • A: "My name is Sarah."
  • Immediate Feedback: The system corrects errors and provides brief explanations to reinforce learning.
  • Application Tip: Use this stage to consolidate theoretical learning, ensuring students practice phrases and structures in a controlled environment.

3. Guided Dialogue: Ready-Made Examples

Objective: Show a practical and visual example of how to apply the learned content in a real dialogue.

How It Works:

  • A prepared dialogue between Robot Samuel and a student is displayed, simulating a complete conversation on the unit's theme.
  • Students read and listen to the dialogue, following the pronunciation and speech rhythm.
  • Example Dialogue:
    • Samuel: "What is your favorite sport?"
    • Student: "I like basketball. And you?"
    • Samuel: "I like football. Do you play often?"
  • Application Tip: Use the dialogue as inspiration for students to create their own dialogues and practice in pairs or groups.

4. Robot Samuel: AI in Real Conversation

Objective: Provide a real conversational experience where the student applies everything learned in the unit.

How It Works:

  • Robot Samuel initiates the conversation personally, addressing the student by name and adapting the dialogue to their English level and unit theme.
  • Follows a script based on the theoretical lesson content but allows spontaneous responses from the student, simulating a real conversation.
  • Didactic Features:
    • Personalization: Samuel recognizes the student's name, level (according to CEFR), and progress.
    • Level Adaptation: Uses short and simple sentences for beginners and more complex sentences for advanced students.
    • Educational Correction: If the student errs, Samuel suggests trying again. Persistent errors result in providing the correct answer and encouraging phrases like "Good try! You're improving!"
    • Constant Motivation: Praises correct answers and maintains a positive and encouraging tone.
  • Example Interaction:
    • Samuel: "Let's begin the lesson, Sarah! What is your favorite hobby?"
    • Student: "I like painting."
    • Samuel: "That's wonderful! Do you paint every weekend?"
    • Student: "Yes, I do."
    • Samuel: "Great! You're doing really well. Let's try another question."
  • Closure:
    • Samuel: "Congratulations, Sarah! You are ready for the next stage. Press the black button to go back."

Why Robot Samuel Is a Powerful Didactic Resource

  • Individualized Practice: Each student practices at their own pace without pressure, ensuring a personalized learning experience.
  • Realistic Simulation: Interaction with Samuel helps students feel more confident practicing English in real-life situations.
  • Instant Feedback: Samuel corrects errors politely and encourages the student to continue, creating a safe and motivating environment.
  • Complete Integration: Robot Samuel connects all previous stages (theory, exercises, and examples), ensuring a smooth transition between learning and practice.

How Samuel Conducts the Conversation

  • Example Interaction:
    • Samuel: "Let's begin the lesson, Sarah! What is your favorite hobby?"
    • Student: "I like painting."
    • Samuel: "That's wonderful! Do you paint every weekend?"
    • Student: "Yes, I do."
    • Samuel: "Great! You're doing really well. Let's try another question."
  • Closure:
    • Samuel: "Congratulations, Sarah! You are ready for the next stage. Press the black button to go back."

Tests: Assessing Student Progress

Tests are essential stages in the teaching-learning process, functioning as evaluation and diagnostic tools that allow monitoring of student progress and consolidation of learning. In our course context, tests play a significant pedagogical role, ensuring that students are prepared to advance to the next unit while providing teachers with a detailed view of each student's performance.

Pedagogical Importance of Assessment

Verify Comprehension

  • Tests assess whether the student has understood the vocabulary, grammar, and concepts covered in the unit.
  • Questions are structured to measure specific skills such as listening, reading, writing, and oral comprehension.

Reflection and Consolidation

  • Answering questions revisits the learned content, reinforcing knowledge and consolidating skills.
  • Tests help students identify their strengths and areas needing more attention.

Direct Feedback

  • Test results provide clear feedback on performance, motivating students to seek improvements.

Development Control

  • Establishing a minimum passing grade (70%) ensures that students advance only after mastering the unit's content.

Monitoring via Teacher Dashboard
