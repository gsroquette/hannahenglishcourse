<h2>Interactive Activity: "The Garden of Choices"</h2>

<h3>Objective:</h3>
<p>Students will practice <strong>Present Simple (affirmative, negative, and interrogative)</strong> in a contextualized way, reflecting on choices, obedience, and consequences.</p>

<h2>Step 1: Preparing the Activity</h2>

<h3>Materials:</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Two cardboard trees placed on the wall:
    <ul>
      <li>One for <strong>"Good Fruits"</strong> (good actions - fruits of the spirit: love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, faithfulness, gentleness, self-control - <strong>Galatians 5:22-23</strong>).</li>
      <li>Another for <strong>"Forbidden Fruits"</strong> (negative actions - acts of the flesh: lying, disobedience, selfishness, envy, pride, disrespect, laziness, ingratitude - <strong>Galatians 5:19-21</strong>).</li>
    </ul>
  </li>
  <li>Cards with images and phrases from the inspirational text.</li>
  <li>Paper fruits that students will create, containing sentences about the story or their daily routines.</li>
  <li>A poster with the question: <strong>"What choices do we make every day?"</strong></li>
</ul>

<h2>Step 2: How to Play</h2>

<h3>"Planting the Seed" - Introduction</h3>
<p>The teacher briefly presents the story of Adam and Eve, showing images.</p>
<p>Asks students: <em>"What choices did Adam and Eve have?"</em></p>
<p>Students discuss and mention examples of daily choices.</p>

<h3>"Harvesting Fruits" - Building Sentences</h3>
<p>Each student receives colored paper to create fruits.</p>
<p>They must write correct sentences in <strong>Present Simple</strong> (interrogative and affirmative or negative response) about the story or their daily routines. Depending on the response, the fruit will be placed on the correct tree (<strong>Good Fruits</strong> or <strong>Forbidden Fruits</strong>).</p>

<h3>Examples:</h3>
<h4>Good Fruit:</h4>
<ul>
  <li><em>Do you always tell the truth?</em> → <strong>Yes, I always tell the truth to my parents.</strong></li>
  <li><em>Does she help her friends at school?</em> → <strong>Yes, she helps her friends every day.</strong></li>
  <li><em>Do we respect our teachers?</em> → <strong>Yes, we respect our teachers every day.</strong></li>
  <li><em>Does God love us even when we make mistakes?</em> → <strong>Yes, God loves us always.</strong></li>
</ul>

<h4>Forbidden Fruit:</h4>
<ul>
  <li><em>Does he listen to his parents?</em> → <strong>No, he does not listen to his parents.</strong></li>
  <li><em>Does Eve obey God's command?</em> → <strong>No, Eve disobeys God's command.</strong></li>
  <li><em>Do they share their toys?</em> → <strong>No, they never share their toys.</strong></li>
  <li><em>Does she steal money from her friend?</em> → <strong>Yes, she steals money sometimes.</strong></li>
</ul>

<h3>Complete List of Keywords for Sentence Formation:</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Subjects:</strong> I, You, He, She, It, We, They, Adam, Eve, The serpent, God, My family.</li>
  <li><strong>Auxiliary Verbs:</strong> do, does.</li>
  <li><strong>Main Verbs:</strong> listen, obey, disobey, help, lie, steal, love, trust, respect, work, eat, give, share, pray, read, study, go, wake up, sleep, talk, follow, choose, forgive, serve.</li>
  <li><strong>Complements:</strong> to my parents, to God, the truth, every day, at school, in the morning, at night, before breakfast, after lunch, in the Bible, in the church, with kindness, with love, with patience, with honesty.</li>
  <li><strong>Time Expressions:</strong> always, sometimes, never, every day, in the morning, in the evening, at noon, on weekends.</li>
</ul>

<h3>"The Garden of Choices" - Quick Debate</h3>
<p>The teacher presents situations for students to reflect on, such as:</p>
<ul>
  <li><em>"You see a friend cheating on a test. What do you do?"</em></li>
  <li><em>"Your parents ask you to help at home, but you want to play. What do you choose?"</em></li>
  <li><em>"Someone is being bullied at school. Do you help or ignore?"</em></li>
  <li><em>"You find money on the floor. What do you do?"</em></li>
  <li><em>"Your friend lies to the teacher. Do you tell the truth?"</em></li>
  <li><em>"A new student has no friends. Do you talk to them?"</em></li>
</ul>
<p>Students respond using <strong>Present Simple</strong> and justify their answers.</p>

<h2>Step 3: Reflection Moment</h2>
<ul>
  <li>The teacher asks questions such as:</li>
  <ul>
    <li><em>"Why is obedience important in our lives?"</em></li>
    <li><em>"How does this story relate to our daily choices?"</em></li>
  </ul>
  <li>Each student writes a sentence about a good choice they can make this week.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Extra Challenge:</h2>
<p>Students create short dialogues in <strong>Present Simple</strong> between two characters discussing a choice. Then, they act it out for the class.</p>

<h2>Conclusion:</h2>
<p>This activity connects grammar, reflection, and interactivity, making learning engaging and meaningful!</p>
