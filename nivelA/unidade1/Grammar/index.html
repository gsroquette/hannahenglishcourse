There is / There are - David and Goliath

A

Look at these sentences from the story of David and Goliath:

  • There is a giant in the valley.
  • There are five stones in David's hand.
  • There is an Israelite army ready for battle.
  • There are many Philistine soldiers.

B

There is and There are are used to say that something exists or is present.

There is = singular (one thing)

  • There is a sling in David's hand.
  • There is a young shepherd named David.

There are = plural (more than one thing)

  • There are five stones.
  • There are many soldiers in the army.

C

Now complete these sentences from the story:

1. __________ a giant named Goliath. (There is)

2. __________ five smooth stones. (There are)

3. __________ a sling in David's hand. (There is)

4. __________ many soldiers in the Israelite army. (There are)

5. __________ a great battle in the valley. (There is)

E

Questions and Answers:

1. What is there in David's hand?

- There is a sling and there are five stones.

2. Who is there in the valley?

- There is a giant named Goliath.

3. How many soldiers are there in the Philistine army?

- There are many soldiers.
