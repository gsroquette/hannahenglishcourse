There is / There are - Negative and Interrogative Forms

A

Look at these sentences from the story of David and Goliath:

  • Negative: There isn't a giant in the camp.
  • There aren't five swords in David's hand.
  • There isn't an Israelite soldier who is as big as Goliath.
  • There aren't any stones left in the stream.
  • Interrogative: Is there a giant in the valley?
  • Are there five stones in David's hand?
  • Is there an Israelite army ready for battle?
  • Are there many Philistine soldiers?

B

1. There _________ any giant in the Israelite camp.

2. There _________ any stones left in David's hand.

3. There _________ a sword in David's hand.

4. There _________ any Philistine soldiers who can defeat Goliath.

5. There _________ any hesitation in David's actions.

6. There _________ a battle at night.

7. There _________ any fear in David's heart.

8. There _________ any doubts about God\'s power.

9. There _________ any large shields in David\'s possession.

10. There _________ a single warrior in Israel who could face Goliath.
